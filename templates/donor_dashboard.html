🩸 Donor Eligibility

To ensure the safety of both the donor and the recipient, certain eligibility criteria must be met before donating blood. Please review the following guidelines carefully:

✅ Basic Eligibility Criteria

  • Age must be 18 to 65 years
  • Minimum weight should be 50 kg
  • Hemoglobin level must be at least 12.5 g/dL
  • Body temperature should be normal
  • Pulse rate must be regular (60–100 beats per minute)
  • Blood pressure should be within normal range

🚫 Temporary Restrictions

You may not be eligible to donate blood if you:

  • Have fever, cold, or infection in the past 7 days
  • Have undergone major surgery in the last 6 months
  • Have recently received vaccination (within 14–28 days depending on type)
  • Are pregnant or breastfeeding
  • Have consumed alcohol within the last 24 hours
  • Got a tattoo or piercing in the last 6 months

❌ Permanent Restrictions

You are not eligible to donate if you:

  • Have HIV/AIDS
  • Have Hepatitis B or C
  • Have serious heart disease
  • Have chronic kidney or liver disease
  • Have blood clotting disorders

❤️ Important Note

  • A donor can donate whole blood every 3 months (90 days).
  • All donated blood is tested and handled following medical safety standards.
  • Your honest disclosure ensures the safety of patients in need.
